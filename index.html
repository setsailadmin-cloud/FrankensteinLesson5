The Epistolary Network

Narrative Machinery in Frankenstein

In Chapter 7, Mary Shelley uses **letters** as structural engines. Because the novel is limited to Victor's first-person perspective, letters are the essential vehicles by which the narrator relates historical developments and tragedies he did not witness directly.

Key Chapter VII Discoveries:
  • ✦ Alphonse's Dispatch: Forces Victor to return to Geneva by conveying the sudden, shocking strangulation of young William.
  • ✦ Elizabeth's Secret Pain: Elizabeth blames herself for the murder because she allowed William to wear their mother's valuable miniature necklace.

Active Focus: The Blindness of Human Justice Workspace

How does Elizabeth's passionate testimony in Chapter 8 inadvertently seal Justine's tragic fate?

Examine how the courtroom spectators reframe Elizabeth's benevolence. Discuss how societal prejudice and mob logic twist acts of pure love into tools of deeper condemnation.

"When she uttered these words, a simple murmur of approval went through the crowd... but it was quickly twisted into hatred." (Chapter VIII) — Highlights how the court turned the family's kindness into an accusation of monstrous betrayal.

Thesis Formula: Elizabeth's defense of Justine backfires because the court operates on emotional bias rather than rational justice, proving that institutional prejudice twists innocent testimony to confirm pre-existing verdicts.
